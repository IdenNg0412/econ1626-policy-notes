# AI Labour Market Policy Proposal

## Overview
This repository contains a policy proposal prepared for the ECON1626 assessment on the economic impacts of artificial intelligence (AI) on labour markets. The report evaluates how AI-driven task automation, augmentation, and task creation reshape employment, wages, industrial structure, and labour market inequality. It also develops and evaluates policy responses aimed at supporting workforce transition and protecting workers from harmful AI-driven employment practices.

The proposal is addressed to the Deputy Secretary, Employment & Skills, Department of Employment and Workplace Relations (Australia).

## Policy Focus

The proposal evaluates three major policy options:

1. Reskilling and Lifelong Learning Programs  
2. Wage Insurance and Labour Adjustment Support  
3. Ethical AI Regulation in Workplaces  

The report recommends an integrated policy package combining workforce development, social protection, and ethical AI governance to support inclusive labour market transition during AI adoption.
