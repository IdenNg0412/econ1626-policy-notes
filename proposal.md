# Problem & Context
Artificial Intelligence (AI) represents a hybrid form of innovation, which can function incrementally as an optimization tool that amplifies existing capability to enhance efficiency across a wide range of industrial applications (Randieri 2025). At the same time, it serves as a disruptive force thanks to its generative capabilities in producing text, images, and music based on user prompts that not only help to improve creative productivity but also transform the nature of work (Randieri 2025). 
## Task automation 
The AI-driven task automation process operates by collecting and analyzing large volumes of data from diverse sources, which enables algorithms to identify relevant patterns and make decisions while continuously improving their performance through each iteration, all without the need for human intervention (Reddy and Roy 2025). AI tools can be integrated into existing business systems seamlessly to perform tasks with greater speed, consistency, and accuracy than human labor, particularly in repetitive and codifiable areas (Filippi et al. 2023). Economically, these AI tools allow businesses to save money by decreasing reliance on human labor while simultaneously boosting productivity, as they can perform activities continuously without needing breaks like humans, leading to changes in how human values are conceived in the labor market (Filippi et al. 2023).

# Analysis of Impacts 
From a geopolitical perspective, the impact of AI on jobs varies across countries, which creates new forms of inequality depending on each country’s ability to innovate, adopt, and adapt and the social acceptance of automation (Alam 2026). Where advanced and emerging economies like the U.S. and China with more substantial AI investments, proactively prioritize innovations and expose human labor to readiness to adopt gains the most from AI efficiency and innovation (Alam 2026). While developing economies like the Latin America region face structural barriers due to weak digital infrastructure and lack of readiness, like inadequate skills and insufficient social protection has widening of inequality and poverty between countries (Parliament of Australia 2025).
