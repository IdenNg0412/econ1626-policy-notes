# Problem & Context
Artificial Intelligence (AI) represents a hybrid form of innovation, which can function incrementally as an optimization tool that amplifies existing capability to enhance efficiency across a wide range of industrial applications (Randieri 2025). At the same time, it serves as a disruptive force thanks to its generative capabilities in producing text, images, and music based on user prompts that not only help to improve creative productivity but also transform the nature of work (Randieri 2025). 
## Task automation 
The AI-driven task automation process operates by collecting and analyzing large volumes of data from diverse sources, which enables algorithms to identify relevant patterns and make decisions while continuously improving their performance through each iteration, all without the need for human intervention (Reddy and Roy 2025). AI tools can be integrated into existing business systems seamlessly to perform tasks with greater speed, consistency, and accuracy than human labor, particularly in repetitive and codifiable areas (Filippi et al. 2023). Economically, these AI tools allow businesses to save money by decreasing reliance on human labor while simultaneously boosting productivity, as they can perform activities continuously without needing breaks like humans, leading to changes in how human values are conceived in the labor market (Filippi et al. 2023). This results in a reduction of labor demand in predictable and standardized occupations, such as administrative and clerical roles, which contributes to short-term unemployment and labor market adjustments that place the greatest pressure on middle-skill workers whose tasks are most vulnerable to substitution (Stephany 2026).
## Task Augmentation
The integration of AI across industries is not limited to task automation but also involves task augmentation, where AI functions as a complementary tool that enhances human productivity and decision-making rather than fully replacing workers (Grimaldo 2025). Through collaborative AI-human partnership, AI performs data-intensive and repetitive tasks while providing analytical insights and recommendations, enabling workers to focus on strategic and complex tasks that require creativity, judgment, and interpersonal skills (Lardi 2025). However, the benefits of AI augmentation are distributed unevenly across the workforce, as higher-skilled workers with advanced digital capabilities combined with specialized skills and training in technical domains are better positioned to leverage AI tools effectively, resulting in greater productivity gains and wage increases compared to lower-skilled workers (Uppili 2026). Consequently, AI-driven augmentation may intensify existing skill and wage inequalities within the labor market, as those without access to advanced training and digital resources may struggle to compete in AI-enabled economy (Alam 2026).

# Analysis of Impacts 
From a geopolitical perspective, the impact of AI on jobs varies across countries, which creates new forms of inequality depending on each country’s ability to innovate, adopt, and adapt and the social acceptance of automation (Alam 2026). Where advanced and emerging economies like the U.S. and China with more substantial AI investments, proactively prioritize innovations and expose human labor to readiness to adopt gains the most from AI efficiency and innovation (Alam 2026). While developing economies like the Latin America region face structural barriers due to weak digital infrastructure and lack of readiness, like inadequate skills and insufficient social protection has widening of inequality and poverty between countries (Parliament of Australia 2025).
